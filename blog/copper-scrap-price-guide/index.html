Types of Copper Scrap

Not all copper scrap is valued equally. Scrap dealers and recyclers classify copper into distinct grades based on purity, condition, and form. Understanding these grades is the first step to getting a fair price.

Bare Bright Copper

This is the highest grade of copper scrap. Bare bright copper consists of clean, uncoated, unalloyed copper wire or bus bar — typically 14 gauge or thicker — with no solder, paint, or insulation attached. It has a bright, shiny appearance and commands the top price in the market.

Common sources include stripped electrical wiring from renovations, heavy-gauge copper cable from construction sites, and clean copper tubing that has been cut and cleaned.

#1 Copper

Number 1 copper includes clean copper tubing, bus bars, clippings, and wire that are free from paint, solder, and heavy oxidation. Unlike bare bright copper, it may have minor discolouration or light patina, but it must still be clean and uncoated. This grade fetches the second-highest price.

Old plumbing pipes, clean radiator tubing, and thick copper strips from electrical installations typically fall into this category.

#2 Copper

Number 2 copper has a lower copper content due to the presence of solder, paint, coatings, or moderate oxidation. It includes items like painted copper pipes, soldered copper fittings, and copper with thin tin coatings. The price is lower than #1 copper due to the additional processing needed to refine it.

Roofing copper, old guttering, and copper with minor attachments generally fall into the #2 category.

Insulated Copper Wire

Insulated copper wire — found in electrical cables, appliance cords, and industrial wiring — is priced based on the copper recovery rate, which depends on the ratio of copper to insulation. Thicker cables with more copper relative to plastic insulation command higher prices.

Many sellers wonder whether to strip the insulation before selling. In general, thick cables (above 2.5mm²) are worth stripping, while thinner household wires may be more economical to sell as-is. Your scrap dealer can advise based on the specific cable type.

Copper Alloy / Brass

Brass — an alloy of copper and zinc — is also commonly found in household and industrial scrap. Brass taps, valves, fittings, decorative items, and certain plumbing components contain significant copper content. While brass fetches less per kilogram than pure copper, it's still a high-value scrap material.

Other copper alloys like bronze (copper and tin) are also recyclable and carry good value. Separating copper alloys from pure copper ensures you get accurate pricing for each material.

Factors That Affect Copper Scrap Prices

Copper scrap prices are not fixed — they change frequently based on several interconnected factors. Understanding these can help you time your sales better and negotiate more effectively.

Global Commodity Markets

Copper is traded globally on exchanges like the London Metal Exchange (LME) and the Multi Commodity Exchange (MCX) in India. International prices set the baseline for local scrap rates. When global demand rises — driven by construction booms, electric vehicle production, or infrastructure projects — copper scrap prices in Hyderabad also increase.

Purity and Grade

As described above, the grade of your copper directly impacts the price. Bare bright copper fetches the highest rate, while #2 copper and insulated wire command lower prices due to the additional processing required. Ensuring your copper is clean and properly sorted is the simplest way to get a better rate.

Quantity

Larger quantities of copper scrap typically command better per-kilogram rates. If you have only a few kilograms, consider accumulating more before selling, or combine your copper with other metal scrap for a consolidated pickup. Dealers like G D Enterprises handle everything from small household lots to bulk industrial quantities.

Current Demand

Local demand from copper smelters, wire manufacturers, and fabrication units influences scrap prices. During periods of high manufacturing activity — such as the pre-monsoon construction season or festive periods — demand for recycled copper rises, pushing prices up.

Local Market Conditions

Transportation costs, the number of active buyers in the area, and seasonal supply variations all play a role. Hyderabad's position as a major industrial and IT hub ensures a relatively strong and consistent demand for copper scrap year-round.

How to Get the Best Price for Copper Scrap

Maximising the value of your copper scrap requires a bit of effort and knowledge. Here are proven tips from our years of experience in the Hyderabad scrap market.

Separate Your Copper by Grade

Mixed copper lots always fetch lower prices than sorted ones. Take the time to separate bare bright copper from #1 copper, #2 copper, and insulated wire. Keep brass and other copper alloys in a separate pile. This simple step can increase your total payout by 10–20%.

Strip Insulation from Thick Cables

For thick cables (above 2.5mm²), stripping the insulation to reveal the bare copper significantly increases value. However, for thin household wiring, the labour may not justify the marginal price improvement — it's often more practical to sell those as insulated wire. Never burn insulation off cables — it's illegal, hazardous, and reduces copper quality.
