Household Plastic Items

Accepting all household plastic waste, furniture, and daily use items

Household Plastic We Buy

  • Furniture & Storage: Plastic chairs, tables, cabinets, wardrobes
  • Kitchen Items: Containers, utensils, jars, bottles
  • Bathroom Items: Buckets, mugs, basins, storage boxes
  • Toys & Games: Broken toys, play items, sports equipment
  • Garden Items: Pots, planters, garden chairs, hose pipes
  • Appliance Parts: Plastic components from old appliances
  • Daily Use Items: Hangers, baskets, organizers, holders

What We Don't Accept

  • Medical waste and contaminated plastics
  • Hazardous material containers
  • Heavily degraded or burned plastic
  • Non-recyclable composite materials

How to Prepare Your Plastic Waste

  1. Clean and wash plastic items to remove food residue
  2. Remove non-plastic parts (metal, paper labels)
  3. Sort by type if possible (bottles, containers, bags)
  4. Crush bottles to save space
  5. Store in dry place until pickup
  6. Call us when you have sufficient quantity (minimum 5kg recommended)

Environmental Impact

By recycling plastic with us, you contribute to:

  • Ocean Protection: Prevent plastic from reaching waterways
  • Energy Savings: Recycling uses 88% less energy than new production
  • CO2 Reduction: Lower carbon emissions significantly
  • Resource Conservation: Reduce dependence on crude oil
  • Landfill Reduction: Keep plastic out of dumps for 450+ years
  • Job Creation: Support local recycling industry

Quick Tips for Maximizing Value

  • Collect plastic over time to reach bulk quantities
  • Keep different types separated for better rates
  • Store in covered area to maintain quality
  • Remove caps and labels when possible
  • Clean thoroughly to get premium prices
  • Coordinate with neighbors for combined pickup
