Air Conditioner Units

Premium prices for old and broken AC systems

AC units contain valuable copper coils, aluminum components, and other metals. Whether your AC is window-mounted, split, central, or portable – working or broken – we offer competitive prices and free pickup service across Hyderabad.

Air conditioning scrap recycling has become a critical environmental service in tropical climates like Hyderabad, where AC usage is nearly year-round. As technology advances and energy efficiency standards improve, homeowners and businesses regularly upgrade their cooling systems, creating a substantial stream of discarded units. Each AC unit represents a valuable collection of recyclable materials—primarily copper coils that can constitute 15-20% of the unit's weight, aluminum fins and frames, steel cabinets, and complex electronic control systems.

The copper content in AC units makes them particularly valuable in the scrap market. Copper prices fluctuate based on global commodity markets, but consistently remain one of the highest-value recyclable metals. Split AC systems typically contain separate indoor and outdoor units, with the outdoor compressor unit housing the most valuable copper components. Window AC units, while more compact, still pack substantial copper in their condensing coils and motor windings. We've developed specialized expertise in evaluating different AC types to ensure you receive fair compensation reflecting current copper market rates.

Beyond metal recovery, proper AC disposal addresses environmental concerns related to refrigerants. Older AC units may contain R-22 (Freon) or other chlorofluorocarbons that damage the ozone layer if released into the atmosphere. Regulations mandate proper refrigerant recovery and disposal, and our certified technicians follow all environmental protocols. We safely extract refrigerants using specialized equipment before processing units for metal recovery, ensuring compliance with environmental standards while protecting our atmosphere.

The condition of your AC unit significantly impacts its value, but even completely non-functional units have worth. Working AC systems might find second markets through refurbishment, commanding premium prices. However, units with failed compressors, refrigerant leaks, or electrical issues still contain valuable metals that retain full scrap value. We evaluate each unit individually, considering factors like age, brand, size, and functionality to provide accurate quotes before pickup.

Commercial AC systems and central air conditioning installations represent high-value recycling opportunities. These large-capacity systems contain substantially more copper and aluminum than residential units, with multi-ton commercial systems weighing hundreds of kilograms. Office buildings, hotels, restaurants, and commercial complexes upgrading their HVAC systems can partner with G D Enterprises for complete system removal. We coordinate with building management to schedule non-disruptive removal, handle all disconnection work, and ensure sites are left clean and ready for new installations.

Timing can matter when selling AC scrap. Copper and aluminum prices experience seasonal and economic fluctuations. Additionally, summer months see increased AC replacement activity, potentially affecting supply-demand dynamics in local scrap markets. We provide transparent, real-time pricing based on current commodity rates, ensuring you always know the fair market value of your equipment. Contact us for immediate quotes that reflect today's market conditions.

Refrigerators & Freezers

Cash for your old fridge or freezer units

Old refrigerators and freezers contain valuable metals and materials. We handle all models – from single-door to multi-door units, broken or working. Our team ensures safe removal and proper recycling of all components.

Refrigerator recycling involves more complexity than most appliance disposal due to refrigerants, insulation materials, and diverse component types. Modern refrigerators are sophisticated appliances containing copper compressors, aluminum evaporators, steel cabinets, plastic interiors, glass shelves, and electronic controls. Each component requires specific handling to maximize material recovery while meeting environmental regulations. G D Enterprises has invested in the knowledge and equipment necessary to process refrigerators responsibly, protecting both the environment and your financial interests.

The compressor unit in refrigerators represents the most valuable single component, containing tightly wound copper windings and significant steel housing. A typical refrigerator compressor weighs 5-10 kg and can constitute a substantial portion of the unit's scrap value. Larger commercial refrigeration units and multi-door models have correspondingly bigger compressors, increasing their overall value. We carefully extract and evaluate compressors separately to ensure you receive proper compensation for this high-value component.

Refrigerant handling requires certification and specialized equipment. Both older refrigerants (like CFCs) and newer alternatives (like HFCs) must be properly recovered before appliance processing. Releasing these substances violates environmental laws and contributes to climate change. Our certified technicians use recovery equipment to capture refrigerants, which are either recycled for reuse or properly destroyed, depending on type and condition. This environmental responsibility doesn't cost you anything—it's included in our service as part of being responsible corporate citizens.

Brand and build quality significantly affect refrigerator scrap value. Premium brands like Samsung, LG, and Whirlpool often use higher-grade materials—thicker steel, more copper in coils, and better-quality compressors. Stainless steel exterior refrigerators command premium prices due to the stainless steel's scrap value. Conversely, budget models with lighter construction still have value but typically receive lower quotes. Our evaluation considers these factors to provide brand-specific pricing that reflects actual material content.

Commercial refrigeration equipment from restaurants, grocery stores, and laboratories represents specialized disposal opportunities. Walk-in coolers, reach-in refrigerators, display cases, and industrial freezers contain substantially more materials than household units. These commercial applications often involve regular equipment turnover as businesses upgrade or close. We provide commercial refrigeration removal services including unit disconnection, refrigerant recovery, and transportation, handling all technical aspects so you can focus on your business operations.

Old refrigerators consume substantially more electricity than modern energy-efficient models, sometimes costing hundreds of rupees monthly in excess power consumption. Upgrading to a new refrigerator and selling your old unit to us creates double savings—reduced electricity bills and immediate cash payment. Many customers find that the combination of energy savings and scrap value helps offset new appliance costs significantly.

Washing Machines & Dryers

Best prices for old laundry equipment

Washing machines contain valuable metals and components. We buy front-load, top-load, fully automatic, and semi-automatic models. Broken machines are also welcome – we extract materials responsibly.

Washing machine recycling addresses one of the most common appliance replacement scenarios in households. As families upgrade to more efficient models, fully automatic systems, or larger capacity units, old washing machines accumulate. These appliances represent significant material value beyond their functional life. A typical washing machine contains 15-25 kg of steel in its drum and cabinet, copper in motor windings and wiring, aluminum in certain components, and various plastics. The electric motor alone can be worth substantial amounts due to its copper content.

Front-loading versus top-loading machines present different value profiles. Front-load washers typically feature more robust construction with heavier drums, larger motors, and more complex mechanical systems—translating to higher scrap values. These machines also tend to be premium products when purchased, using better-quality materials throughout. Top-load machines, while simpler, still contain valuable metals and represent good recyclable value, particularly in fully automatic variants with electronic controls.

The washing machine motor represents a concentrated source of copper, one of the highest-value recyclable metals. Motor windings contain pure copper wire, while the motor housing provides steel. Industrial and commercial washing machines used in laundromats, hotels, or hospitals feature particularly robust motors designed for heavy-duty cycles, making them especially valuable in scrap recycling. We recognize these differences in our evaluations, offering premium rates for commercial-grade equipment.

Even broken washing machines retain full material value. Common failure modes—blown motors, cracked drums, faulty electronics, or broken transmissions—don't diminish the intrinsic metal content. Some customers hesitate to sell non-functional machines, assuming they're worthless. In reality, we're primarily interested in the materials, not functionality. A machine that won't spin or agitate still contains the same amount of steel, copper, and aluminum as a working unit, maintaining its scrap value.

Washer-dryer combination units and standalone dryers also fall within our purchase scope. Dryers contain heating elements, substantial steel drums, motors for tumbling action, and electronic controls. Combination units offer the material value of both appliances in a single footprint. These space-saving machines popular in urban apartments have particular appeal as scrap due to their complexity and material content.

For property managers, hospitality businesses, and senior living facilities with multiple laundry machines, we offer fleet disposal services. When renovating laundry facilities or upgrading equipment, disposing of multiple machines simultaneously creates logistics challenges. Our team coordinates bulk pickups, handles all removal logistics, provides per-unit pricing transparency, and completes transactions efficiently to minimize disruption to your operations.

Other Home Appliances

We buy various household appliance items

Beyond the major appliances, we buy various household items containing valuable materials. Every appliance in your home has scrap value – turn it into cash with G D Enterprises!

The modern household accumulates numerous electrical appliances beyond the major ones—each containing recyclable materials with intrinsic value. While individually these items may seem insignificant, collectively they represent substantial waste streams and recovery opportunities. Microwave ovens contain copper transformers and magnetrons, along with steel cabinets. Water heaters (geysers) house copper heating elements and substantial steel tanks. Dishwashers feature stainless steel interiors, copper wiring, and electric pump motors. Each appliance category has specific material profiles that our experts evaluate for fair compensation.

Small kitchen appliances—toasters, kettles, blenders, food processors—might seem too minor for scrap value, but their cumulative impact matters. These items contain heating elements (often nichrome or copper-based), small motors, steel housings, and various metals. While individual unit values may be modest, households often accumulate multiple such appliances over time. We encourage bundling smaller appliances together for pickup, making the transaction worthwhile for both parties. Ten small appliances together can equal the scrap value of one larger item.

Water purification systems and air purifiers represent increasingly common household investments in Hyderabad's urban environment. These units contain various metals in pumps, UV chambers, filters, and housings. RO water purifiers feature electrical pumps with copper motors, while air purifiers contain fans, HEPA filter frames, and electronic controls. As these systems require periodic replacement—typically every 5-10 years—they enter the waste stream regularly. Rather than discarding them, selling to G D Enterprises recovers material value while ensuring proper disposal.

Vacuum cleaners, particularly older models, contain substantial copper in their powerful suction motors. Commercial or industrial vacuum systems used in offices, hotels, or manufacturing facilities feature even more robust motors and metal construction. We purchase all vacuum types—upright, canister, wet-dry, and robotic models—recognizing that each design incorporates recyclable components. Even units with clogged motors or broken hoses maintain their material scrap value.

Television sets bridge categories between electronics and appliances, containing complex circuit boards, speakers, power supplies, and either LCD/LED panels or older CRT tubes. The electronics recycling aspect of televisions requires careful handling due to components like backlighting systems (containing mercury in older models) and circuit boards with trace amounts of precious metals. We've developed specialized expertise in television recycling, managing both modern flat panels and legacy CRT units that still linger in storage across many households.

Seasonal appliance turnover creates predictable disposal patterns. Summer months see increased AC and refrigerator upgrades, while monsoon seasons prompt replacement of damaged appliances. Understanding these patterns, we maintain capacity to handle peak disposal periods while offering consistent pricing and service quality year-round. Whether you're doing spring cleaning, renovating your home, or simply decluttering, G D Enterprises provides a reliable outlet for appliance disposal with fair compensation.
